    About Georgia

    About Georgia

    Rich Cultural Heritage: Georgia has a rich and ancient cultural heritage, with a history dating back thousands of years. The country is known for its UNESCO World Heritage Sites, such as the historic districts of Tbilisi and Mtskheta, the cave city of Uplistsikhe, and the medieval monasteries of Gelati and Jvari. Visitors can explore ancient fortresses, churches, and traditional villages, experiencing the unique Georgian culture and traditions

    *****

    Wine and Gastronomy

    Wine and Gastronomy: Georgia is one of the oldest wine-producing regions in the world, with a history of winemaking that spans over 8,000 years. The traditional Georgian winemaking method using qvevri (large clay vessels buried underground) has been recognized by UNESCO as an Intangible Cultural Heritage. Visitors can indulge in wine tastings, vineyard tours, and authentic Georgian cuisine, which featuresdelicious dishes like khachapuri (cheese-filled bread) and khinkali (dumplings).

    *****

    Adventure Tourism

    Adventure Tourism: For thrill-seekers, Georgia offers a range of adventure activities. You can go hiking in the stunning mountain trails of Svaneti or Kazbegi, try paragliding over the scenic landscapes, or go skiing in world-class resorts like Gudauri and Bakuriani. The country also offers opportunities for horseback riding, rafting, and rock climbing.

    *****

    Film Tourism:

    Film Tourism: Georgia has gained popularity as a filming location for international movies and TV series. The breathtaking scenery and unique architecture have attracted productions like "Game of Thrones," "The Chronicles of Narnia," and "Fast & Furious 9." Film enthusiasts can visit the filming locations and explore the settings of their favorite movies and shows

    *****

    Hospitality:

    Hospitality and Warmth: Georgians are known for their exceptional hospitality and warmth towards visitors. The concept of "Georgian Supra" reflects the traditional feast where guests are treated with great respect and generosity. This cultural aspect creates a welcoming and friendly atmosphere for tourists, enhancing their overall experience in the country.

    *****

    Ease of Travel

    Ease of Travel: Georgia offers a relatively straightforward visa policy for many nationalities, with visa-free entry for citizens of numerous countries. The country has modern transportation infrastructure, including international airports in Tbilisi and Kutaisi, and an extensive network of highways and railways, making it convenient to travel within the country.

    *****

    Sustainable Tourism: Georgia is increasingly focused on sustainable tourism practices, aiming to preserve its natural and cultural heritage. There are initiatives promoting eco-friendly accommodations, community-based tourism projects, and responsible travel experiences that contribute to the local economy and support local communities. These are just a few highlights of Georgia's tourism potential. The country continues to evolve as a popular destination, offering a unique blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and warm hospitality to visitors from around the world.
